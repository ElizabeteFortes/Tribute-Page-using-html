Martin Luther King

1929 - 1968


"I have the audacity to believe that peoples everywhere can have three meals a day for their bodies, education and culture for their minds, and dignity, equality, and freedom for their spirits."

- Martin Luther King


Timeline- Key moments in the Civil Rights Movement:

1929 - Martin Luther King, Jr., born in the segregated south of Atlanta, Georgia. He was born Michael Luther King, Jr., but later had his name changed to Martin.
1954 - Segregation in public schools is prohibited by the U.S. Supreme Court.
1955 - A bus boycott begins in Montgomery, after Rosa Parks, an African American woman, is detained on December 1st for refusing to give up her seat to a white person.
1956 - Martin Luther King house was bombed by segregationists in retaliation for the success of the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
1957 - Garfield High School becomes the first Seattle high school with a more than 50 percent nonwhite student.
1960 - The sit-in protest movement begins in February at a Woolworth and spreads across the nation.
1961 - Freedom rides begin from Washington. Groups of black and white people ride buses through the South to protest.
1963 - 250,000 people attend the March on Washington, D.C. urging support for pending civil-rights legislation.
1964 - President Johnson signs the Civil Rights Act. Martin Luther King, receives the Nobel Prize.
1965 - President Johnson signs the Voting Rights Act.
1968 - Martin Luther King, was shot dead while standing on a balcony outside his second-floor room at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee.
